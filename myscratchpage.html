Larry Bird: The Legend of French Lick

The "Hick from French Lick"

Larry Bird is considered one of the greatest basketball players of all time because of his elite shooting, clutch performances, and instinctual passing. Larry grew up in French Lick, Indiana where he developed a love of basketball playing with other African Americans in the town. He truly broke barriers in doing so and was differen for his time.

Larry Bird entered the NBA at age 23 and was drafted from Indiana State to play for the Boston Centics. Bird excelled there and helped the team make the playoffs in a few seaosns. In 1981, Bird won his first championship with the Celtics and went on to play against the Los Angeles Lakers in the ensuing years, beating them in NBA Finals in 1984 and 1986

Larry was a talented shooter and often called his shots before they went in. He was also a big trash talker often beating down on opponents physcially and mentally. He always seemed to be one step ahead of the defense and knew exactly where he needed to be to hit the game winning shot, make the game winning passing, or steal the ball to save a game.

Bird's Major Accomplishments

  1. Winner of 3 NBA Championships (1981, 1984, 1986)
    • 1984 Finals MVP
    • 1986 Finals MVP
  2. Three Consecutive NBA MVPs (1984-1986)
  3. 12-time NBA All-Star
